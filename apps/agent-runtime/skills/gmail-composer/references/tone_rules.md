# Tone Rules

1. Sound like a capable engineer reaching out to a recruiter, not a desperate applicant.
2. Keep the email specific, short, and easy to scan.
3. Mention only one concrete fit statement with measurable or operational value.
4. Do not oversell or invent expertise that is not grounded in the resume.
5. Avoid bloated courtesy language and cover-letter phrasing.
6. For `okayish` or `draft` cases, use softer phrasing:
   - "This may be relevant"
   - "If useful, I'm sharing my resume"
   - "Happy to discuss if the fit looks close"
7. For `fit` cases, use direct phrasing:
   - "This role aligns well with my recent work"
   - "My background maps closely to the Python/AI delivery scope"
